\section{Question 10.5.2.13}
How many 3 digit numbers are divisible by 7?
\section{Solution}

\input{tables/table1}


Three digit numbers which are divisible by 7 are \(105, 112, 119, \ldots, 994\), which form an arithmetic progression (A.P). 
The number of terms in the AP x\brak{n} is given by: 
\begin{align}  \label{eq:eq1}
    n = \frac{x\brak{n} - x\brak{0}}{d}  + 1
\end{align}
\begin{equation}
    a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    X(z) = \frac{x[0]}{1 - z^{-1}} + d\frac{z^{-1}}{(1 - z^{-1})^2} \quad |z| > 1  \label{eq:eq2}
\end{equation}

 
\begin{align}
x\brak{n} &= \brak{105 + \brak{n}7}u\brak{n}
\label{eq:eq3}
\end{align}
Using the values in \tabref{tab:table0} :
\begin{align}
    k = \frac{994 - 105}{7} + 1 = 128
\end{align}
Taking z transform of \eqref{eq:eq3} :
\begin{align}
 X\brak{z} = \frac{105 - 98z^{-1}}{\brak{1-z^{-1}}^2} \quad |z| > 1 
\end{align}
